The CNMI forms what is best known as the Marianas Archipelago. It is made up of the 14 northernmost islands in the western Pacific Ocean. It is a remote island community in the Pacific with a big story. Many people do not know much about the islands, which is full of culture, history, and an identity that makes the islands special and unique.
Dive deeper into some interesting facts about the Marianas!

People & Culture

The Marianas is home to the indigenous Chamorro and Carolinian people. The cultures are heavily influenced by other cultures including the Spanish and Japanese cultures. These influences can be seen in the language, dishes, and traditions. Get a glimpse of the traditional Chamorro and Carolinian dances

Environment

The Marianas is surrounded by natural beauty across the islands. From its rich green forests, to its tranquil beaches with diverse coral reefs, the islands have a unique environment with a variety of places to visit.

History

After being colonized by Spain, Germany, and Japan, the CNMI played a key role during World War II. The United States used the islands as a strategic base for launching attacks against Japan. Following the war, the US took control of the islands in 1944, and then officially became a territory in 1978.

Colonial Timeline

  • Spanish Period (1521-1899)
  • German Period (1899-1914)
  • Japanese Period (1914- 1944)
  • United States Commonwealth (1944-present)

Climate & Weather

The Marianas has a tropical climate all year round. There are only 2 main seasons that occur in the Marianas, the Dry Season that usually occurs from December to June, and the Rainy Season that usually occurs from July to November.
Visiting the Marianas? Plan ahead of the rainy season to enjoy more outdoor adventures and activities!
